Fundamental Analysis of Global Currency Markets

What is Fundamental Analysis?

Fundamental analysis (FA) is a method of assessing a currency's value based on macroeconomic indicators, political events, and other factors that influence a country's economy. Unlike technical analysis, which focuses on charts and indicators, fundamental analysis examines the underlying reasons for currency price fluctuations.

Fundamental analysis helps traders:

  • Predict long-term trends in the currency market;
  • Determine the impact of economic news on exchange rates;
  • Choose optimal entry and exit points in the market;
  • Analyze central bank policies and their influence on the market.

How Does Fundamental Analysis Affect Currency Markets?

Every national currency is influenced by multiple economic and political factors. For example, high inflation in a country can lead to a depreciation of its national currency, whereas an increase in interest rates by the central bank can strengthen the currency.

Key macroeconomic indicators affecting the currency market:

  • Central bank interest rates;
  • Inflation rate and Consumer Price Index (CPI);
  • Employment and labor market data (NFP, unemployment rate);
  • Gross Domestic Product (GDP);
  • Trade balance and balance of payments;
  • Geopolitical situation and economic stability.

Interest Rates and Their Impact on Currency Markets

Interest rates are one of the key factors influencing exchange rates in global markets. Central banks regulate interest rates to manage inflation, stimulate economic growth, and maintain financial stability. Changes in interest rates directly impact the supply and demand for a currency.

How Do Interest Rates Affect Currency Value?

When a central bank raises interest rates, it makes the national currency more attractive to investors, as deposits and bonds denominated in that currency offer higher returns. As a result, demand for the currency increases, strengthening its exchange rate.

Conversely, lowering interest rates reduces the appeal of the currency for investors, leading to its depreciation. Thus, currency markets react to interest rates as follows:

Traders can use several strategies when analyzing interest rates:

The Impact of Inflation on Currency Exchange Rates

Inflation is one of the key macroeconomic indicators influencing currency values. It reflects the rate of price increases for goods and services in an economy. High or unstable inflation can weaken a currency, while moderate and predictable inflation levels contribute to economic stability and strengthen the national currency.

The relationship between inflation and exchange rates can be explained as follows:

  • High inflation reduces the purchasing power of a currency, making it less attractive to investors. As a result, the currency depreciates.
  • Low inflation or slowing inflation makes an economy more stable and predictable, which supports currency appreciation.

If inflation exceeds acceptable levels, central banks may respond by adjusting interest rates, which in turn affects the currency's value.
